About

I am a committed research follow, working at the Warwick Manufacturing Group of the University of Warwick. Before this, I was a doctoral researcher at the University of Manchester (UoM) working on developing stochastic model predictive control schemes for multi-energy systems with high penetration of electric vehicles. I hold a PhD in Electrical and Electronics Engineering, which was completed in the control and power systems research group at the University of Manchester.

You can look through my work experience.

Work Experience

Research Fellow

Warwick Manufacturing Group
(research and educational institution)
November 2021 -- Present

Main tasks include:

  • Built and commissioned a thermal management system based on non-linear model predictive control for a 440V thermal rig for a commercial electric van using MATLAB/Simulink and Vector real-time system.
  • Calibrated and commissioned a 440V thermal rig which is capable to emulate a vehicle EDU, battery and cabin thermal condition in different climate and control settings, establishing communication with a PC using Python and Vector real-time simulator.
  • Developed a control-oriented model of a thermal refrigeration unit and collaborated with stakeholders, such as CoolVan limited and GAH limited, to define the requirements and specifications for the prototype and its sub-systems.

Doctoral Researcher

University of Manchester
(research and educational institution)
November 2017 -- October2021

Main tasks include:

  • Adapted a novel generalized control-oriented modelling framework for multi-energy systems to consider uncertain generation and demand sources.
  • Developed a successive electric vehicle charge/discharge operation model considering uncertain user behaviour, tailoring it to be implementable in both online deterministic and stochastic predictive control algorithms.
  • Implemented stochastic programming techniques to account for uncertainty in the forecasts for electric vehicle characteristics, availabilities and charging preferences.
  • Collaborated with CROSSBOW (CROSS BOrder management of variable renewable energies and storage units enabling a translational Wholesale market) on optimising a systematic approach to coordinate the storage capacity of electric vehicles considering uncertainties of their characteristics, availabilities and charging preferences.
  • Developed and compared the performance of deterministic and stochastic energy management schemes to counter uncertainties related to electric vehicles as well as other generation and demand sources.
  • Supervised and guided several undergraduate and masters' students towards completing courses and projects on Smart Grids & Sustainable Electricity Systems, PID Control, Model Predictive Control, Micro-controller Engineering, Control Systems.
